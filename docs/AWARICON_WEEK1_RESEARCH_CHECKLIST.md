## **RESEARCH TOOLS & RESOURCES**

### **Finding Emails:**
1. **Clearbit Chrome Extension** (clearbit.com)
   - Install: chrome.com/webstore
   - Use: Visit their website, click extension → finds email

2. **Hunter.io** (hunter.io)
   - Search: "[Name] [Company]"
   - Results show likely email format + confidence score

3. **LinkedIn** (Direct approach)
   - Visit LinkedIn profile
   - Look for: Email in "Contact Info" or website link
   - Fallback: DM the person directly

4. **Company website:**
   - [Company]/contact page
   - [Company]/about → team page → click names for email
   - [Company]/careers → often has team directory

### **Finding Recent News:**
1. **Google News:** 
   - Search: "[Organization Name] 2025 2026"
   - Filter: "Past Week" or "Past Month"

2. **LinkedIn:**
   - Search organization OR person
   - Look at recent activity/posts

3. **Organization website:**
   - Visit: /blog or /news section
   - Look for: Recent press release or announcement

4. **Twitter/X:**
   - Search: "@[organization]" or "#[topic]"
   - Filter: "Latest" or "Past Week"

### **Finding Contact Pages:**
- [organization].com/about
- [organization].com/contact
- [organization].com/team
- [organization].com/careers (usually has team info)
- LinkedIn company page → "See Jobs" often shows staff structure

## **COMMON RESEARCH MISTAKES (Avoid)**

❌ Using generic org email (like hello@company.com)  
✓ Use specific decision-maker email

❌ Connecting with random company staffer on LinkedIn  
✓ Connect with decision-maker directly

❌ Using "person named X" generically in email  
✓ Reference their specific recent work (article, interview, announcement)

❌ Sending same email to all orgs  
✓ Personalize 80% for each org's context

❌ Email opens with "we've built something cool"  
✓ Email opens with "I was reading [their thing]..."
