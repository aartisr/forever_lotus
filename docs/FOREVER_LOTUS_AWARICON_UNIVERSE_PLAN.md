## 2) Manifesto Compliance: Non-Negotiable Constitutional Constraints

All execution must satisfy these hard rules, derived from MANIFESTO and MANIFESTO_EVALUATOR:

1. No dark patterns.
2. No manipulative fear-based acquisition messaging.
3. Privacy-first data model and minimal extraction.
4. Accessibility-first product and content production.
5. Empowerment over dependency in client delivery.
6. Public impact reporting with evidence and uncertainty disclosure.
7. Human-AI transparency in all generated deliverables.

### 2.1 Compliance Gate for Every Initiative

Any initiative enters execution only if it passes all seven checks:

1. Compassion Check: Does it reduce suffering or confusion?
2. Dignity Check: Does it increase user agency?
3. Stewardship Check: Is resource use responsible and efficient?
4. Transparency Check: Are assumptions and trade-offs disclosed?
5. Inclusion Check: Is it accessible and culturally adaptive?
6. Evidence Check: Can claims be traced to data?
7. Reversibility Check: If harmful, can we roll back quickly?

If any check fails, initiative is blocked.

## 7) Causality Model and Experimental Design

### 7.1 Primary Causal Chain

1. Evidence credibility increases trust.
2. Trust increases conversion and retention.
3. Retention funds product and standards investments.
4. Product and standards increase adoption.
5. Adoption increases visibility and citation.
6. Citation loops back to evidence credibility.

### 7.2 Experiment Classes

1. Message experiments: value framing by vertical.
2. Offer experiments: pilot structure and incentives.
3. Product experiments: integration friction and activation time.
4. Governance experiments: review cadence and decision quality.

### 7.3 Statistical and Practical Rules

1. Pre-register experiment hypotheses and stop rules.
2. Use minimum detectable effect thresholds before rollout.
3. Require practical significance, not only statistical significance.
4. Publish major experiment outcomes in transparency digest.

3. Institutional skepticism due to novelty.

1. Canonical definitions and standards papers.

3. Team bottlenecks in high-dependency workflows.

Countermeasures:

1. Manifesto drift routine and gate enforcement.
2. Data quality score and reconciliation routine.

## Reputational Risks

2. Perception of moral branding without rigor.

Countermeasures:

2. Publish limitations and correction logs.
